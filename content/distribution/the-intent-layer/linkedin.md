A reframe that feels more accurate to me now:

React made frontend work declarative by moving us above manual DOM mutation.

I think prompts, specs, and validation loops are starting to do the same thing for engineering work.

This is basically the synthesis of the last few posts in the series.

The important artifact is not the one-line prompt.

It is the intent layer around the task:

- behavior
- constraints
- scenarios
- boundaries
- proof

A prompt can carry some of that.
A spec can stabilize it.
Validation makes it operational.

That last part matters.

Without validation, the declaration is just aspiration.
With validation, an agent can compare the running product against the spec and keep iterating until reality matches.

That is why I care less about clever prompt wording and more about specification quality.

Prompts start the interaction.
Specs define the system.
Validation closes the loop.

That combination feels much closer to declarative engineering than "prompting" in the shallow sense.
