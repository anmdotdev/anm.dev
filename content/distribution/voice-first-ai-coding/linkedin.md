The most useful thing voice changed in my AI workflow was not speed.

It was context density.

I type fast. Faster than I speak cleanly.
So if this were only about words per minute, voice would not matter much.

But that is the wrong comparison.

The real problem with typed prompts is that they make you compress too aggressively.

You know:

- where the relevant code already lives
- what pattern the change should follow
- which edge case is likely to break
- what absolutely should not regress

Then you type something like:
"add a dark mode toggle to the header"

That is not a thinking failure.
It is a transmission failure.

Voice changes that because saying the extra sentence feels cheap.
And then the extra sentence after that.

So the model gets more of the context that was already in your head.

That is why I think voice-first prompting works.
Not because talking is magical.
Because it lowers the cost of being detailed.

AI rewards clarity more than brevity.
Voice helped me stop confusing those two things.
