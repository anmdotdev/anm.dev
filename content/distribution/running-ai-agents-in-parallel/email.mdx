The first time I ran three agents in parallel, it looked slightly ridiculous: three terminals, three copies of the repo, three streams of work moving at once.

What made it valuable was not the spectacle. It was the compounding effect of a workflow that was already disciplined enough to support concurrency.

> Parallelism is not the first trick. It is the last one.

If prompts are vague, feedback loops are weak, and review is sloppy, more agents just multiply chaos. Parallel work only helps once the single-agent loop is already trustworthy.

- Give each agent an isolated workspace.
- Parallelize bounded tasks, not architectural uncertainty.
- Keep one lane for validation or review, not only implementation.

The surprising bottleneck is not usually the models. It is you. Your ability to frame the work, answer questions, review output, and merge decisions becomes the ceiling.

That is why three agents feels like a sweet spot for me. Enough parallel flow to stay efficient, not so much that review quality collapses. Throughput only matters if trust stays high.
